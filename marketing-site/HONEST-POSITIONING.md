# Honest Positioning: Upstream vs. Adonis Intelligence

## Summary of Content Updates

I've rewritten the competitor comparison content to remove aspirational marketing claims and focus on what Upstream **actually does today** vs. what's on the roadmap.

---

## What Was FALSE (Removed):

### ❌ "Detect risk 2-4 weeks before denials appear"
**Reality:** Upstream analyzes post-submission denial data weekly. This is the same data Adonis uses, just with a slower cadence.

### ❌ "Early-warning behavioral drift detection before claims are submitted"
**Reality:** Upstream analyzes claims **after** submission, not before. Pre-submission risk scoring is on the roadmap but not built.

### ❌ "Prevention-focused: alerts before submission, not after"
**Reality:** Both products analyze post-submission outcomes. Neither prevents denials before submission currently.

### ❌ "Specialty-specific rules for nephrology, dental, family practice, ABA, imaging"
**Reality:** These features are **on the roadmap (Q1-Q2 2026)** but not built yet. Current product does general denial detection.

### ❌ "DriftWatch™ detects payer behavioral changes 2-4 weeks before denials appear"
**Reality:** DriftWatch™ is weekly analysis of already-submitted claims. It's **slower** than Adonis (7 days vs 24-48 hours).

---

## What IS TRUE (Kept/Updated):

### ✅ Payment Timing Monitoring (DelayGuard)
**Honest claim:** "DelayGuard monitors when payers slow down (e.g., 45 days → 52 days). Payment timing changes are leading indicators that often precede denial rate changes."

This is **actually different** from what Adonis does and provides genuine early-warning value.

### ✅ 30-Day Implementation vs. 3-6 Months
**Honest claim:** "Upstream is EHR-independent (CSV/clearinghouse upload). Adonis requires deep EHR integration (athenahealth, AdvancedMD, eCW). Implementation: 30 days vs 3-6 months."

This is verifiable and differentiating.

### ✅ Transparent Fixed Pricing vs. Percentage of Recovery
**Honest claim:** "Upstream: Fixed monthly fee ($1,500-$3,500). Adonis: Percentage of recovery (variable, not disclosed). Budgeting: predictable vs unpredictable."

This is a real advantage.

### ✅ Weekly Analysis Reduces Alert Fatigue
**Honest claim:** "Upstream's weekly cadence filters out one-off issues and focuses on systemic trends. Trade-off: slower than Adonis (7 days vs 24-48 hours), but reduces daily noise."

This reframes "slower" as a feature (reduced alert fatigue) while being honest about the trade-off.

### ✅ Specialty-Focused Roadmap
**Honest claim:** "Upstream is building specialty-specific features for 1-5 location practices (nephrology, dental, family practice, ABA, imaging). Roadmap Q1-Q2 2026. ABA authorization expiration tracking is Q1 priority."

This is honest about timeline and doesn't claim features exist today.

## Honest Competitive Differentiation

### Choose Adonis If:
- You need **real-time alerts (24-48 hours)**
- You're on athenahealth, AdvancedMD, or eCW and want embedded workflow
- You're okay with 3-6 month implementation and percentage-based pricing

### Choose Upstream If:
- You need **fast implementation (30 days) and transparent pricing**
- You want **payment timing monitoring** (leading indicator for payer policy changes)
- You prefer **weekly trend analysis** over daily alert noise
- You're willing to wait for **specialty features (Q1-Q2 2026)** while using general denial detection
- You use an unsupported EHR or want EHR-independent deployment

4. **Weekly analysis reducing alert fatigue** reframes "slower" as a feature while being honest about trade-offs
